CRM Customer Onboarding End-to-End Knowledge

Objective

The CRM onboarding application is designed to ensure that HSBC only establishes relationships with customers who meet regulatory, compliance, and internal risk requirements.

The onboarding process follows the principle:

Know Your Customer (KYC) → Assess Risk → Obtain Approvals → Activate Customer → Open Products

The application supports:

* Customer onboarding
* Customer maintenance
* KYC review
* Regulatory compliance
* AML controls
* FATCA/CRS
* Risk assessment
* Investment account onboarding

⸻

Departments involved

When a customer wants to open an investment account, several teams work together.

Manager-Level Summary

If your manager asks:

“Explain CRM Customer Onboarding.”

You can answer:

“CRM onboarding is a controlled process that ensures HSBC establishes business relationships only with customers who satisfy regulatory, AML, KYC, and internal risk requirements. The process begins with collecting customer information and identifying the customer type, legal structure, ownership, and business activity. We then identify shareholders, Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs), controllers, and authorized signatories, and verify their identities. The customer and all connected parties undergo sanctions, PEP, AML, and adverse media screening. FCC-RAM calculates the customer’s inherent risk using factors such as geography, industry, ownership, products, and delivery channels. If the customer falls into a high-risk category, Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) and additional approvals are required. FATCA/CRS information is collected for tax compliance. Once all compliance, risk, and approval requirements are satisfied, the customer is activated in CRM, and the required banking and investment products are enabled.”
